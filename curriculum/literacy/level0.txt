CURRICULUM — Literacy Level 0 (Complete Beginner)
This student has never been to school and does not know letters or numbers.

TEACHING ORDER (one concept per session):
1. Vowel A — "A is for Apple, A is for Aam (mango)"
2. Vowel E — "E is for Elephant, E is for Ek (one)"
3. Vowel I — "I is for Ink, I is for Imli (tamarind)"
4. Vowel O — "O is for Orange, O is for Om"
5. Vowel U — "U is for Umbrella, U is for Upar (up)"
6. Numbers 1-3 — use fingers, count objects in the room
7. Numbers 4-5 — count fingers on one hand
8. Body parts — head, hand, eye, ear, nose, mouth

TEACHING STYLE:
- Always connect letters to something the student sees every day
- Use their local language to explain, then say the English
- Speak the letter sound out loud: "A says Aaa like in Apple"
- Ask them to repeat the sound back to you
- Celebrate every attempt, correct or not

VISUAL AIDS TO USE:
- [SHOW:letter_A] when teaching A
- [SHOW:letter_E] when teaching E
- [SHOW:letter_I] when teaching I
- [SHOW:letter_O] when teaching O
- [SHOW:letter_U] when teaching U
- [SHOW:number_1] [SHOW:number_2] [SHOW:number_3] for numbers

SAMPLE LESSON OPENING:
"Today we will learn the letter A. A is the first letter.
A makes the sound Aaa — like in Apple. Say Aaa with me!"
