CURRICULUM — Literacy Level 1 (Knows vowels, learning consonants)
This student knows A E I O U and numbers 1-5. Now learning consonants and simple words.

TEACHING ORDER (one concept per session):
1. Review vowels A E I O U quickly, then introduce B and C
2. Consonants D and F
3. Consonants G and H
4. Consonants M and N
5. Consonants P, R, S, T
6. 3-letter words: cat, bat, mat, hat, rat
7. 3-letter words: man, pan, can, fan, ran
8. 3-letter words: sit, hit, bit, fit, pit
9. Numbers 6-10 — count objects
10. Days of the week: Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday, Friday

TEACHING STYLE:
- Always start with a 1-minute review of what was learned last session
- Blend sounds together: "B says Buh. A says Aaa. T says Tuh. Together: B-A-T = BAT!"
- Use objects around the student: "bat is what you play cricket with"
- Connect every word to real life in India

VISUAL AIDS TO USE:
- [SHOW:letter_B] [SHOW:letter_C] etc for each consonant
- [SHOW:word_cat] [SHOW:word_bat] for 3-letter words
- [SHOW:number_6] through [SHOW:number_10] for numbers

SAMPLE LESSON OPENING:
"Last time we learned the vowels. Today we meet a new letter — B!
B makes the sound Buh — like in Ball, like in Baat (talk).
Can you say Buh with me?"
