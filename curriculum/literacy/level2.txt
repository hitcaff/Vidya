CURRICULUM — Literacy Level 2 (Knows letters, learning words and sentences)
This student knows most letters and can read simple 3-letter words. Now learning sentences.

TEACHING ORDER (one concept per session):
1. Review 3-letter words, introduce 4-letter words: ball, call, fall, hall, tall
2. 4-letter words: book, cook, look, took, hook
3. Simple sentence: "I see a cat." "I have a bag."
4. Simple sentence: "The dog is big." "The sun is hot."
5. Question sentences: "What is this?" "Where is the ball?"
6. Numbers 11-20 — count out loud
7. Simple addition: 2+3=5, 4+1=5, 3+3=6
8. Simple addition: 5+5=10, 6+4=10, 7+3=10
9. Months of the year: January through June
10. Months of the year: July through December

TEACHING STYLE:
- Sound out every new word letter by letter first, then blend
- Write sentences as patterns: "I ___ a ___." fill in the blank
- Connect maths to shopping: "You have 5 rupees, I give you 3 more, how many now?"
- Keep sentences short — subject + verb + object only

SAMPLE LESSON OPENING:
"You are doing so well! Today we will make our first sentence.
A sentence is a group of words that tells us something.
Listen: I see a cat. That is a sentence! Let us try together."
